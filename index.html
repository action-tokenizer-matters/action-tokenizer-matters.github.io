Action Tokenizer Matters in In-Context Imitation Learning

IROS 2025

An Dinh Vuong1     Minh Nhat Vu2     Dong An1     Ian Reid2

1MBZUAI   2University of Adelaide
arXiv Code

Abstract

In-context imitation learning has recently emerged as a promising paradigm for decision-making by conditioning large models on demonstration sequences. However, we show that the choice of action representation — the “action tokenizer” — significantly influences performance across multiple tasks. In this paper, we systematically analyze how different tokenization schemes affect the in-context learning ability of transformer-based policies. We propose an improved action tokenizer that incorporates kinematic priors and demonstrate its superiority in robotic manipulation tasks, outperforming baselines in both data efficiency and generalization.

Highlights

  • We demonstrate that action tokenization is a critical factor in in-context imitation learning.
  • We propose a new tokenizer design that respects robot kinematics and continuous control smoothness.
  • Our approach improves generalization to unseen tasks and environments.
